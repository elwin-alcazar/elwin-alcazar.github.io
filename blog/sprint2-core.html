About Me

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I want to be a social entrepreneur and empower the underprivileged people from New Zealand and the Philippines. I believe that technology can help bridge these gaps in equality. To realise this goal, I plan to first join a few companies doing Good Tech to learn the ropes and then branch out and start my own.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Based on my character strengths of kindness and fairness, I believe I will be a good team player. I am also analytical and logical which will come handy in problem solving.

As for my limitations, I might struggle to think on my feet as I like to do things in a well thought out manner.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Confidence to speak my mind – I might hesitate to share my opinion because I might feel that I am not an expert hence my words won’t bear any weight.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Since I plan to be a social entrepreneur, I would like my leadership, networking and communication skills to be developed.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect the bootcamp team to foster an environment that is nurturing, fun, and collaborative. I also expect them to provide constructive feedback and be available when I need help. I also hope they can provide valuable insights about the industry and how to be successful in it.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I expect that for the whole duration of the Bootcamp that it will be my no. 1 priority.

I also expect myself to prepare questions for the facilitators for our 1 on 1 meetings.

If I am starting to feel a bit overwhelmed, I will let my facilitator know. Hopefully we can sort things out and be able to determine which tasks I need to prioritise.

Lastly, I expect myself to enjoy my Bootcamp experience but at the same time use this period to also come up with at least a rough plan on the next steps that I will take.

To realise this goal, I plan to first join a few companies doing Good Tech to learn the ropes and then branch out and start my own.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

I am naturally curious so I enjoy learning new things. I also like to be well-prepared and this translates to learning through always ensuring that I have a basic understanding of the current lesson before I move on to a new one. Finally, I am constantly looking for ways to improve, so even in learning, I will try and adapt the most effective methods.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Since I plan to be a social entrepreneur, I would like my leadership, networking and communication skills to be developed.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

  • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives
  • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

As an early bird, I am most productive in the mornings. Thus I intend to allot my mornings solely to doing Dev Academy work. I will also adopt productivity tools such as the Pomodoro Technique to ensure that I am not just working hard, but working smart as well.

In a group setting, I am most effective if I am well-prepared. So for every group discussion or meeting, I promise to prepare in advance so that I am able to contribute meaningfully.

If I am starting to feel a bit overwhelmed, I will let my facilitator know. Hopefully we can sort things out and be able to determine which tasks I need to prioritise.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I learned that solving problems on your own is an effective method to learn. But since time is of the essence for this programme, I know that I need to reach out and ask for help whenever I am stuck. So I am commiting to solve problems on my own first. But if I still am not able to solve it within 30 - 60 minutes, then I will seek help from my colleagues first and then from the facilitators either through Slack or in person.
