How will you engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit?

Now that I know that our brains are malleable, I now also know that I can learn anything I want or create a new habit. Consistency and deliberate practice are the keys for success. I will also be patient since learning slows as we age. And it would be easier for me to learn if I can relate it to my previous experiences.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

  1. Learn new things! Learn how to play a musical instrument, or speak a new language.
  2. Do not cram and get plenty of rest.
  3. Excercise!
  4. Practice mindfulness.

Growth mindset - discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A person who has a growth mindset believes that human capabilities – both intellectual and emotional, can be cultivated through effort and deliberate practice. This means that we can have ambitious goals, and it is possible to achieve them through hard work and commitment.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I’ve learned about growth mindset last year during lockdown when I was reading up on positive psychology and flourishing. This allowed me to have ambitious goals and I know that there is a way for me to achieve them. In fact, if I haven't know about growth mindset, it's possible that I might not have enrolled in this course.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will not be discouraged by failure and will not think that I failed but instead I will frame it as a learning experience. I will also not compare my progress against other people but rather check if I’ve improved from yesterday.
